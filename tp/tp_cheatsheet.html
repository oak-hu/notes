Topology — result · proof sketch · moral

Every key result with the one-move proof idea (↳) and the takeaway (∴). Spines: compactness → "compact→Hausdorff cts bijection is a homeo" → identify quotients, and simplicial complexes → closed combinatorial surfaces → classification.

Point-set foundations
Topological space 1.1, 1.6 Result: opens closed under finite ∩ & arbitrary ∪; a metric gives one via ε-balls. openness is declared; (T2) is finite only — infinite ∩ of opens needn't be open. keep the open sets, drop the distance — that's the whole move of topology.
Continuity; homeomorphism 1.20–1.24 Result: f cts ⟺ f−1(open) open (⟺ f−1(closed) closed). complements swap open↔closed; compositions of cts are cts. continuity needs only open sets; sequential-cts is weaker in general spaces.
Separation: T1 / Hausdorff 1.46–1.52 Result: T1 ⟺ singletons closed; Hausdorff ⇒ unique limits. separate distinct points by disjoint opens. Hausdorff is the hypothesis that powers "compact⇒closed" & quotient arguments — cofinite is T1 but not Hausdorff.
Closure · interior · ∂ 1.27–1.44 Result: x∈Ā ⟺ every open nbhd of x meets A; interior is the dual. Ā = smallest closed superset; X∖Å = closure of X∖A. pointwise test always; sequences capture closure only in metric spaces (1.35).
Subspace · basis · product 1.54–1.73 Result: product basis {U×V}; (f,g) cts ⟺ both components cts. test continuity on a basis; not every product-open set is a rectangle. the product topology is built to make projections cts (the coarsest that does).
Connectedness
Connected 1.77 Result: connected ⟺ no cts onto {0,1} ⟺ only clopen sets are ∅, X. a disconnection is a cts surjection to {0,1}. the {0,1} criterion proves every connectedness fact in one line.
Image · union · closure 1.80–1.85 Result: cts image of connected is connected; overlapping connected sets union to connected; A⊆B⊆Ā ⇒ B connected. a cts map to {0,1} is constant on each piece and they agree on overlaps. build big connected sets by gluing overlapping ones; X×Y connected ⟺ both.
path-conn ⇒ connected 1.89 Result: path-connected ⇒ connected (converse false). each path-image is connected (cts image of [0,1]); they share the basepoint. paths buy connectedness cheaply; the sine curve is the gap (connected, not p.c.).
Compactness
Compact; FIP 2.3, 2.4 Result: every open cover has a finite subcover ⟺ closed sets with FIP have non-empty ∩. De Morgan dual of "cover ↔ empty intersection." compactness = "a good proxy for finiteness."
closed-in-cpt; cpt⊆Hdff ⇒ closed 2.11, 2.14 Result: closed subset of compact is compact; in a Hausdorff space, compact ⇒ closed. separate an outside point from K by disjoint opens ⇒ complement open. needs Hausdorff — a singleton in the indiscrete space is compact but not closed.
cts image of compact; EVT 2.19, 2.21 Result: cts image of compact is compact ⇒ a cts f:X→ℝ on a compact X attains its bounds. pull a cover back, take a finite subcover, push forward. the existence-of-extrema engine, with no metric needed.
Tychonoff; Heine–Borel 2.23, 2.24 Result: X×Y compact ⟺ both compact; ⊆ℝn compact ⟺ closed + bounded. tube lemma; a box is a finite product of compact intervals. "closed+bounded ⇒ compact" is ℝn-only — false in infinite dimensions.
compact→Hausdorff homeo 2.25 Result: a cts bijection from a compact space to a Hausdorff space is a homeomorphism. it's a closed map: closed→compact (2.11)→compact image (2.19)→closed (2.14). the tool of the course — identifies quotients and pins |K| down to homeomorphism.
seq cpt ⟺ cpt (metric) 2.29–2.36 Result: a metric space is compact ⟺ sequentially compact. Lebesgue number + a finite ε-net ⇒ a finite subcover. metric-only; in general spaces sequences don't see compactness.
Quotient spaces
Quotient topology & maps 3.x, 3.28 Result: U open in X/∼ ⟺ p−1(U) open — the finest topology making p cts. declare opens by their preimages; a quotient map is exactly this. the universal "collapse / glue" construction.
Universal property 3.9 Result: g:X/∼→Z is cts ⟺ g∘p is cts. pull g back through the collapsing map p. how you build (and check) maps out of a quotient.
Hausdorffness of a quotient 3.17–3.21 Result: X/∼ Hausdorff ⟺ distinct classes lie in disjoint open saturated sets. opens of X/∼ ↔ open saturated sets of X. a quotient of a Hausdorff space can fail to be Hausdorff (ℝ/ℚ is indiscrete).
Identify a quotient 3.11, 3.35 Result: X compact, Y Hausdorff, f cts onto ⇒ X/(fibres of f) ≅ Y. f factors through X/∼ as a cts bijection; apply 2.25. the recipe for S1, the torus, S2, RPn from gluings.
Simplicial complexes
Realisation |K|; triangulation 4.5, 4.6, 4.8 Result: glue standard simplices along face inclusions; a triangulation is a homeo |K|→X. |K| is a quotient of a disjoint union of simplices. combinatorial data (vertices + which subsets are simplices) → a space.
|K| of a finite complex is nice 4.21–4.24 Result: finite K ⇒ |K| is compact, metrizable, Hausdorff. cts injection |K|↪ℝN from a compact space into Hausdorff ⇒ homeo onto image (2.25). finite complexes are as well-behaved as subsets of ℝN.
Edge-connected ⟺ connected 4.20 Result: |K| connected ⟺ any two vertices are joined by an edge path. the set of vertices reachable from v spans a clopen subcomplex. connectivity of |K| is a purely combinatorial check.
Surfaces
Polygon ⇒ closed surface 5.1–5.6 Result: a polygon with a complete set of side identifications is a closed surface. build a disc nbhd at each inside / edge / vertex point. a boundary word is all the data you need to specify a surface.
Closed combinatorial surface 5.15, 5.16 Result: every vertex link a circle ⇒ each edge is in exactly two triangles and |K| is a surface. link = circle ⇒ the vertex's star is a disc. the triangulated model that makes "surface" combinatorial & classifiable.
Classification theorem 5.18 Result: every closed surface ≅ exactly one Mg (sphere + g handles) or Nh (sphere + h crosscaps). boundary-word reduction: cancel xx−1, collect crosscaps & handles; handle + crosscap = 3 crosscaps. two infinite families classify all closed surfaces.
Euler characteristic 5.29–5.31 Result: χ = V−E+F is a topological invariant; χ(Mg)=2−2g, χ(Nh)=2−h. count on any triangulation; invariance is deep (Algebraic Topology). χ + orientability distinguish all the surfaces (Mg orientable, Nh not).
